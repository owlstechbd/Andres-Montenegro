Understanding the value of discipline is essential to overcoming failure, and throughout my journey, I have faced more setbacks than successes. However, each loss became a stepping stone to growth, pushing me to become a better man by recognizing my weaknesses and taking immediate action to improve. Discipline became the foundation of who I am today.

My journey began in Costa Rica, where I dreamed of being different and striving for excellence. At the age of 21, I joined the French Foreign Legion, where I faced some of the most grueling physical and mental challenges of my life. Training in the unforgiving terrains of Africa and Europe, I learned the meaning of true resilience and developed a deeper sense of self-awareness that would shape my leadership style.

In 2012, after two years of relentless effort and perseverance, I joined the US Army. The 75th Ranger Regiment became a critical point in my development as a professional, providing me with the tools to refine my leadership skills and become more strategic in my decisions. The Regiment was a place where discipline, teamwork, and accountability were paramount, reinforcing the importance of self-awareness and responsibility in every action taken.

The ultimate test of my physical and mental limits came with the Lancero School, a program known for its extreme challenges. This experience pushed me beyond anything I had encountered before, both physically and mentally, solidifying my resolve to excel as a leader under the most intense conditions.

Throughout this journey, I learned three languages — English, French, and Spanish — entirely on my own, using discipline and self-study as my guides. Today, with almost 18 years of military experience behind me, I am stepping into a new chapter, determined to use what I have learned to empower others to become stronger, more adaptive leaders.

Academic Background

My academic journey began with a Bachelor’s degree in Organizational Leadership, providing a foundation in strategic thinking and effective communication. Continuing with a Master’s degree in Strategic Leadership, the focus shifted to advanced leadership concepts, emphasizing decision-making in complex environments. Currently, the pursuit of a Doctor of Executive Leadership degree centers on developing resilience in leadership through direct feedback, integrating research and practical application to foster adaptive and effective leadership practices in diverse settings.

Career Background

The professional journey has spanned nearly 18 years of military service, with extensive experience in the French Foreign Legion, the 75th Ranger Regiment, and Lancero Colombia. Specialized training includes executive protective service, maritime security, and advanced tactical operations, along with leadership courses focused on high-stakes decision-making, social intelligence, and crisis management. Working in diverse environments has provided a comprehensive understanding of both operational and strategic leadership, emphasizing the importance of adaptability, discipline, and clear communication in challenging situations. These experiences have cultivated a skill set that combines tactical expertise with strategic thinking, preparing for leadership roles that demand quick decision-making and resilience under pressure.

DEL Program Goals

The primary objective within the DEL program is to deepen leadership capabilities by focusing on social intelligence, emotional intelligence, and persuasive communication. Developing strategic thinking and conflict resolution skills remains a priority, aiming to translate lessons from military operations into civilian leadership contexts. Emphasis is placed on using constructive feedback as a mechanism for fostering resilience in both emerging and established leaders. The goal is to not only refine personal leadership skills but also to inspire others to reach their full potential, creating a culture where continuous learning and adaptive leadership are at the forefront of organizational success.

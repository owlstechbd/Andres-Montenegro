Current Leadership Model

The current leadership model is structured around the foundational concept of Resilience Development in Leadership through Direct Feedback, as outlined in the primary literature map. This model emphasizes several key components that serve as pillars for effective leadership:

Psychological Safety

Establishing trust as the cornerstone of leadership communication, creating an environment where constructive feedback is delivered without fear of reprisal. This approach encourages open dialogue, fosters emotional safety, and ensures that leaders and team members can engage in honest, transparent communication.

Read More
Emotional Intelligence

Developing the ability to manage emotions effectively, both in oneself and in others. This includes recognizing emotional triggers, maintaining composure in high-pressure situations, and using empathy as a tool to enhance feedback delivery.

Read More
Authentic Leadership

Integrating transparency, integrity, and authenticity into leadership practices. Leaders are expected to model self-awareness, exhibit genuine care for their teams, and maintain ethical standards, particularly when delivering feedback or navigating adversity.

Read More
Feedback Culture

Promoting a culture where feedback is not only accepted but actively sought after as a tool for continuous learning and adaptation. Leaders are encouraged to adopt a growth mindset, viewing feedback as a pathway to personal and professional development.

Read More
Trust

Cultivating trust through consistent, open communication and demonstrated reliability. Leaders are expected to foster connections that strengthen team cohesion, reinforcing the idea that trust is both the foundation and outcome of effective feedback practices.

Read More
Resilience and Leadership

Emphasizing the importance of adversity as a learning tool. Leaders are trained to view setbacks as opportunities to strengthen their decision-making abilities, using direct feedback as a mechanism for growth and long-term success.

Code of Conduct

Integrity in Action

Every decision and action taken should be grounded in ethical principles, ensuring that trust and honesty remain uncompromised.
Resilience in Leadership

View challenges as opportunities to grow, using setbacks as a source of strength and a catalyst for continuous development.
Accountability as a Mindset

Take full responsibility for outcomes, both positive and negative, and use every experience as a learning opportunity for self-improvement.
Transparency in Communication

Be clear, open, and honest in all interactions, promoting a culture where feedback is constructive, timely, and aimed at fostering growth.
Respect as a Standard

Foster an inclusive environment where diverse perspectives are valued, creating a space where every voice is heard and respected.
Empathy as a Guiding Principle

Recognize the human element in every interaction, using emotional intelligence to connect with others, address conflicts, and build stronger relationships.
